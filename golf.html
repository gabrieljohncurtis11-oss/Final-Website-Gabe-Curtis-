Why I Love Golf

Golf is more than just a game to me — it's one of the best ways to connect with the people around you. There's something special about spending four hours on a course with someone. You're walking, talking, competing, and laughing together in a way that doesn't really happen anywhere else.

Some of my favorite memories have come from a round of golf. Whether it's celebrating a great shot with a friend, trash talking a bad putt, or just enjoying a beautiful morning on the course together — those moments stick with you. Golf has a unique way of slowing life down and giving you real time with the people you care about.

That's what keeps me coming back. It's not just the competition or the challenge of the game — it's the people you share it with and the memories you build along the way.

What Makes Golf Great

  1. The Mental Game
    • Staying calm after a bad shot
    • Visualizing the shot before you swing
    • Managing your emotions across 18 holes
  2. The Technical Craft
    • Club selection and distance control
    • Reading greens and understanding break
    • Shot shaping — draws, fades, and punches
  3. The Experience
    • Beautiful courses and early morning tee times
    • Competing with friends in a casual round
    • The feeling of a perfectly struck iron
  4. The Culture
    • Etiquette and respect for the game
    • Watching the Masters and the majors
    • Following pros like Rory, Scottie, and Scheffler
