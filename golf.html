Why I Love Golf

Golf has a way of humbling you. One great round can make you feel unstoppable, and the next one brings you right back down to earth. That tension — between confidence and humility — is what keeps me coming back.

There's something uniquely satisfying about a sport where you compete against yourself as much as anyone else. Every shot is a small problem to solve: distance, wind, lie, club selection. It rewards preparation, focus, and composure under pressure — skills that honestly translate into everything else in life.

I started playing seriously in high school and haven't stopped since. Whether it's a casual round with friends or grinding out a tight back nine, golf is one of my favorite ways to spend a day.

The perfect swing — a lifetime in the making.

What Makes Golf Great

  1. The Mental Game
    • Staying calm after a bad shot
    • Visualizing the shot before you swing
    • Managing your emotions across 18 holes
  2. The Technical Craft
    • Club selection and distance control
    • Reading greens and understanding break
    • Shot shaping — draws, fades, and punches
  3. The Experience
    • Beautiful courses and early morning tee times
    • Competing with friends in a casual round
    • The feeling of a perfectly struck iron
  4. The Culture
    • Etiquette and respect for the game
    • Watching the Masters and the majors
    • Following pros like Rory, Scottie, and Scheffler
